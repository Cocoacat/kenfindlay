  GRADED WALKS  


Walking grades are provided as a general guide only. If you have any doubt about your fitness for a particular walk then please contact me in advance.

Bear in mind that these are only rough guidelines, don’t forget that the distance of the walk, types of terrain and the possibility of bad weather can make a walk more difficult than planned.

If you're unsure of your fitness level, try a short, easy walk first so you can gauge your own fitness.

 



Easy - walks for everyone, including people with conventional wheelchairs and pushchairs, using easy access paths. Some of these paths may be rough with some short ascents and descents, smooth and hard surfaces with a possibility of some obstructions such as steps and gates. Comfortable shoes or trainers can be worn e.g. Ecup, Swinsty or Grimwith Reservoirs.

Moderate - walks for people with country walking experience and a reasonable level of fitness. May include some steep paths/sections and open country with some hilly ground to contend with. Most of the paths will be well marked but there will be some steep sections to compete with. Walking boots and warm, waterproof clothing are essential e.g. circuit of Ilkley Moor and Rylstone.

Difficult - walks for experienced country walkers with an above average fitness level. This may include hills and rough country, and may be at a brisk pace. Some of the ascents or descents could be quite steep in angle so you must have a god sense of balance. Walking boots and warm, waterproof clothing are essential e.g. Ingleborough or Whernside
