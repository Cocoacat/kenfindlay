Training Packages

Expedition Skills

This course (based in the classroom) covers all the necessary skills needed in order to plan your own expedition and covers two modules listed below

 

Section One:

Investigating a range of different types of expedition’s e.g. individual/group/commercial/scientific/exploratory, benefits of expeditions etc.


Section Two

Planning an expedition to include location, duration, aims, objectives, expectations, route plan, itinerary, kit list, risk assessment, environmental issues, health and safety, camp craft, safe use of stove and fuel, food hygiene and fire risk.

 

One day course £50 per person.

Team building sessions

These activities are designed to get your team to interact with each other and to improve their communication skills and confidence in dealing with others in a practical setting. Catering for teams of up to 10.

 

 Cost half day £30 per person      

Cost full day £50 per person        

 

Activities include:

 

  • The ‘Drainpipe race’ to improve your communication skills, flexibility and hand to eye co-ordination.

  • The ‘Blind trail’ in which you totally trust your colleagues and your own judgement.

  • ‘Sweep search’ designed to create realism and make you work with your team and to take some responsibility for the final outcome.

  • ‘Crossing the toxic swamp’ together with your team you will work out the quickest and safest route across the toxic swamp without getting being penalised.

  • ‘Bomb disposal’ in which your team has only a short space of time in which to work out a way to move the bomb to a safe area before it explodes.

  • ‘Orienteering exercise’ your team will compete to find the checkpoints in the fastest possible time.

Introduction to Map reading

This is designed to give you some basic knowledge about how to interpret and read maps. The course will cover the following topics:-

 

Section One

  • Different scales of maps

  • Map signs and symbols

  • Contours

 

Section Two

  • Using a compass to follow a bearing

  • Four and six figure grid references

  • Completing a route card

 

Section Three

  • Pacing and distance estimation

  • Navigation games.

 

Cost per person £25 per person per session

CHARITY DONATIONS


KENTREK supports the Upper Wharfedale Fell Mountain Rescue Team.


UWFRA is a registered charity. Like other mountain and cave rescue teams in the UK it receives no funding from central Government. The team is run entirely by volunteers and has no paid members of staff.


UWFRA is made up of over 60 well trained and well equipped volunteer cavers, climbers and mountaineers and is on call 365 days a year.Volunteers live in the Dales or travel from as far away as Leeds or Bradford and are prepared to respond to a callout at any time of the day or night in the worst of weather conditions.


 For over 65 years the UWFRA. has been rescuing people and animals from the caves and fells around Wharfedale, Nidderdale, Littondale and Mid-Airedale.
